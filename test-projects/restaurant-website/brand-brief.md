# Restaurant Brand Brief

## Working Concept

**Name:** Ember & Fennel

**Concept:** Ember & Fennel is a modern neighborhood restaurant centered on live-fire coastal Mediterranean cooking. The menu leans on grilled seafood, roasted vegetables, handmade flatbreads, bright herbs, citrus, olive oil, and seasonal market produce. The experience should feel polished but relaxed: strong enough for a date night or celebration, easy enough for a spontaneous weeknight dinner.

**Service model:** Dinner-first full-service restaurant with cocktails, wine, and shared plates. Weekend brunch is a secondary revenue stream, but dinner is the core identity.

**Atmosphere:** Warm, lively, layered, and design-forward without feeling formal or exclusive.

## Target Diners

**Primary audience:** Professionals and couples ages 28-45 looking for a memorable dinner spot for date nights, small group outings, and casual celebrations.

**Secondary audience:** Local residents ages 35-60 who want a reliable upscale neighborhood restaurant for hosting friends, birthdays, and out-of-town guests.

**Audience mindset:**
- They care about ambiance as much as the food.
- They want quality and originality, but not fine-dining stiffness.
- They are willing to pay more for strong design, great cocktails, and a meal that feels special.
- They are likely to discover restaurants through Instagram, Maps, word of mouth, and hotel or local recommendations.

## Price Point

**Tier:** Upper-midscale / approachable special occasion

**Expected spend:**
- Dinner food per person: $35-$55
- Dinner with drinks: $65-$90
- Cocktails: $14-$18
- Wine by the glass: $13-$17
- Shared plates and starters: $12-$22
- Mains: $24-$42
- Weekend brunch: $22-$35 per person

This price point positions the restaurant above casual neighborhood spots, but below formal tasting-menu restaurants.

## Differentiators

1. **Live-fire identity**
Every part of the menu should feel shaped by smoke, char, and open flame, not just a few signature dishes.

2. **Mediterranean flavors through a local seasonal lens**
The restaurant is not a generic "Mediterranean" place. It uses regional inspiration with high-quality seasonal produce and seafood that change through the year.

3. **Design-forward but not intimidating**
The brand should feel visually elevated and editorial, while the service and copy stay warm and welcoming.

4. **Built for social dining**
The menu should naturally support sharing: spreads, flatbreads, small plates, grilled proteins, and vegetable dishes that make the table look abundant.

5. **Neighborhood relevance**
It should feel like a place locals return to, not just a one-time destination restaurant.

## Brand Voice

**Tone:** Warm, sensory, confident, unfussy

**What the voice should do:**
- Use vivid food and atmosphere language
- Sound assured without sounding luxurious for its own sake
- Make the restaurant feel desirable and human
- Balance craft with accessibility

**What the voice should avoid:**
- Fine-dining jargon
- Overly rustic cliches
- Generic phrases like "delicious food" or "something for everyone"
- Corporate hospitality language

## Visual Mood

**Keywords:** ember, olive, brass, stone, linen, citrus, smoke, candlelight

**Art direction notes:**
- Warm neutrals with deeper olive, terracotta, charcoal, and muted gold accents
- Low, flattering light and high-texture surfaces
- Photography focused on flame, plated shareables, cocktails, hands at the table, and crowded intimate dining moments
- Typography should feel editorial and intentional, not default tech or startup-inspired

## Website Implications

The site should emphasize:
- Immediate reservation access
- A strong hero image or statement that communicates atmosphere
- Food and drink menus with seasonal credibility
- A short but distinctive story about the live-fire Mediterranean point of view
- Private dining or group dining options if added later
- Location, hours, and walk-in friendliness for neighborhood traffic

## One-Sentence Positioning

Ember & Fennel is a warm, design-led neighborhood restaurant where live-fire Mediterranean cooking turns dinner into an easy, elevated night out.
